## Quick answer

<!-- ssot_texts | v2026-02-28 09:55 Europe/Berlin | source: categories.csv:body_en_kurzantwort | key:skirting-boards | path_de:produkte/leisten/sockelleisten | path_en:products/mouldings/skirtings -->

- Skirting boards create a clean transition between wall and floor.
- They protect wall surfaces from impact and cleaning stress.
- Profile height should be coordinated with architraves and wall mouldings.
- In period properties, uneven substrates must be considered early.

## Practical context

<!-- ssot_texts | v2026-02-28 09:55 Europe/Berlin | source: categories.csv:body_en_praxis | key:skirting-boards | path_de:produkte/leisten/sockelleisten | path_en:products/mouldings/skirtings -->

Skirting is typically defined after wall and floor decisions, allowing coherent alignment of heights, joints, and transitions even in non-orthogonal existing rooms.

## Decisions & variants

<!-- ssot_texts | v2026-02-28 09:55 Europe/Berlin | source: categories.csv:body_en_varianten | key:skirting-boards | path_de:produkte/leisten/sockelleisten | path_en:products/mouldings/skirtings -->

Common options range from restrained low profiles to taller historic skirting with stronger articulation. Depending on design intent, skirting can stay subtle or become a visible framing element.

## Process & planning

<!-- ssot_texts | v2026-02-28 09:55 Europe/Berlin | source: categories.csv:body_en_ablauf | key:skirting-boards | path_de:produkte/leisten/sockelleisten | path_en:products/mouldings/skirtings -->

Typical process: set reference height, choose profile, plan miters/corners, verify substrate, install, and align finish layers. Transitions to door architraves are resolved in detail beforehand.

## Cost logic

<!-- ssot_texts | v2026-02-28 09:55 Europe/Berlin | source: categories.csv:body_en_kosten | key:skirting-boards | path_de:produkte/leisten/sockelleisten | path_en:products/mouldings/skirtings -->

Main cost drivers are linear meters, profile complexity, number of corners, substrate condition, and finishing adjustments. Many small transitions increase labor effort.

## Common mistakes & how to avoid them

<!-- ssot_texts | v2026-02-28 09:55 Europe/Berlin | source: categories.csv:body_en_fehler | key:skirting-boards | path_de:produkte/leisten/sockelleisten | path_en:products/mouldings/skirtings -->

Frequent errors include inconsistent height lines, poor miter quality, and missing coordination with architraves. Precise layout lines and sample sections prevent rework.
